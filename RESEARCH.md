## 5. Market & regulatory context (the demand engine)

New Jersey law makes fire sprinkler ITM (inspection/testing/maintenance) a **recurring legal obligation** — this is the core content & lead-gen opportunity:

- NJ Uniform Fire Code (Uniform Fire Safety Act, N.J.S.A. 52:27D-192 et seq.; NJ edition of IFC 2015 Ch. 9) mandates ITM of water-based systems **per NFPA 25** (Table 901.6.1; §903.5).
- Systems must be **operative at all times** (§901.4.1).
- **NFPA 25 cadence:** weekly/monthly gauge & valve checks → quarterly waterflow alarm/FDC tests → annual full system inspection → 5-year internal pipe/obstruction inspections. NJ DFS issues standardized quarterly and annual sprinkler inspection report forms.
- **NJ-specific record rule:** ITM records kept on premises 3 years AND forwarded to the local fire official **within 5 business days** (§901.6.2) — a paperwork burden contractors handle for clients; marketable service.
- Backflow preventers: annual test (§901.6.4). NJ placarding of hydraulically calculated systems (Form F380, §903.6).
- **Only state-certified contractors may do this work** (N.J.S.A. 52:27D-25q) — legal moat; "hire only a NJ-certified contractor (Permit #P01570)" is a converting message.
- **Owner penalties for non-compliance:** $500–$5,000 per violation per day, up to $25,000 for life-safety violations, possible closure and insurance cancellation — the fear/urgency driver for commercial customers.
- Typical customer profile in this market: **commercial & institutional** (offices, manufacturing, healthcare, restaurants, schools, multi-family, warehouses) — property managers and building owners with compliance anxiety.
